Thesis Research Revisited

Published: Wed 16 August 2017
By Chris Cox

In Science.

tags: Thesis Greenland Snow Heat Conduction

For my Master’s degree I studied glacial hydrology at the University of Wyoming. Glacial hydrology is basically trying to figure out how water moves above, below, and through glaciers and ice sheets, and what effects that water has on things like sliding, melting, erosion, and water chemistry. The work involved several projects(some of which failed), side experiments, tangents, and eventually a thesis.

I had a lot of fun in grad school and did some interesting scientific/technical explorations. It is kind of shocking to realize it has already been 5 years since I finished my degree. I can’t imagine very many people have read my thesis since then, and I don’t like the idea that something I worked hard on is simply lost in some digital dustbin. Thus begins a multi-part series detailing different aspects of my thesis. Some of the topics may be useful to others involved in numerical computing while others I would just like to revisit and try to explain better to a general audience.
